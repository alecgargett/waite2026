My plan

Hi. I'm Alec Gargett and I have a plan.

My plan aims to:

  • Solve the cost of living crisis while protecting and improving our quality of life, our environment, the beauty of our city and our suburbs, and our public services.
  • Solve the algae crisis, and the pollution crises that are causing it.
  • Solve the health care crisis, including the ramping crisis.
  • Solve the underemployment crisis. Ensure that every Australian is supported to make the best use of their skills, talent and knowledge.
  • Solve the corruption crisis. Deliver integrity in politics, transparency and accountability in government and a stronger democracy, where politicians serve and represent their communities and pursue the interests of their electorate, not a separate agenda of a political party or corporations.
  • Protect our children, women and men from harm, including from harmful advertising like gambling adverts, and from unfair treatment. Within the limits of our political system, I'll do what I can to protect all individuals from injustice.

Big claims? Yes. But at least I have a plan. At least I'm going to try.

Sometimes it seems like most of our politicians and political parties aren't even trying. They prefer to play around the edges of our problems; throw a lot of money at problems without solving their causes; or choose policies that mitigate some problems while making other problems worse. Sometimes they choose a hopeless political strategy to boot, and for almost all political parties it's a divisive strategy.

My plan involves a comprehensive set of policy solutions, respect for the whole community, and a pragmatic strategy to support a democratic political movement beyond our own electorate here in Waite, and beyond state politics, to influence politics for the better at the federal and international levels. We need better policies and we need a better way of doing politics, and if I'm elected I'll use the platform of state parliament to do my part in both.

Some of the detail of my policies will be released on December 22 of this year, some may be released earlier, some later, not only here on the website, but in video form on platforms like Facebook, Instagram and Youtube. By the election in March next year, I'll get my plan across to as many people in Waite as I can, and I could use your support. Following me on social media or video platforms so that you can see my policy videos and perhaps even share them around would be a great help. So would sharing this website with whoever you think might be interested.

Best wishes,
Alec Gargett

Solving the underemployment crisis

The official (low hours) underemployment rate is 5.9%, and the real underemployment rate may be well over 20%. 5.9% is only the number of employed Australians who are working fewer hours than they would like to. It doesn't include the 4.5% of unemployed Australians who are seeking a job, which makes a total of more than 10% of Australians who would like more hours of work than they have. Even this combined figure doesn't include all of the 20% of Australians who are not fully using their qualifications, not to mention the unknown number of Australians whose skill and knowledge are not being put to best use, meaning that the true underemployment rate, in the broader sense, may be well over 20%.

Beyond policy

By itself, even the most comprehensive set of policies is not a complete solution without a viable strategy. To get good policies implemented, we need to build a political movement that will be taken seriously by the government, and become a part of government. We also need to address what is fundamentally broken in our major parties. If a major party cannot be fixed, it needs to be replaced, by independents, and if necessary by new political parties, that are designed to hold politicians accountable to their electorates, rather than pushing some other agenda of the party or its donors.

If I'm elected, I won't be resigned to what I can do within state parliament to influence state policy as a single member. I will use the platform of state parliament to support the movement toward a better democracy, which will influence both the state and federal government to do better.
